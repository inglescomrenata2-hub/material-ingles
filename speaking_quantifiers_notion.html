Speaking Practice · B1 · Quantifiers

How many is enough?

A fluency activity to practise quantifiers in real conversation. Work in pairs or answer the prompts out loud on your own.

Quantifiers to use
too many too little too much enough not enough a few a little several hardly any just enough
1
Pick a card and talk for 60 seconds
Choose any card below. Speak about that situation using as many quantifiers as you can.
personal
Your social life this month
How many plans did you have? Did you see enough friends? Were there too many commitments?
work
A project or task at work / study
Did you have enough time? Too many people involved? Too little support?
music
Your favourite band or artist
How many albums do they have? Too many members or just enough? Enough recognition?
learning
Your experience learning English
Do you have enough practice? Too little speaking time? A few good resources or hardly any?
city
Where you live
Too many people? Enough green spaces? Too little quiet? Hardly any good options for something you need?
tech
Your relationship with social media
Too much scrolling? Hardly any real connection? Enough inspiration or too many distractions?
2
React to these statements — agree or disagree
Use quantifiers in your answer. Give a reason or a personal example.
  • "There are too many streaming platforms today — it's hard to choose what to watch."
  • "Most people don't spend enough time away from their phones."
  • "In Brazil, there are hardly any opportunities for independent musicians to earn a good income."
  • "Online courses give students too little human interaction to really learn well."
  • "Big cities have too many people and too little community."
3
Use these sentence starters
Continue each sentence with your own ideas. Speak for at least 2–3 sentences each.
In my opinion, there are too many… keep going — what problems does this cause?
I feel like I never have enough… why? what would change if you did?
Hardly any of my friends… is that a good or bad thing?
I've noticed that several… give a specific example or story
There's too little…in the world today. what would you do to change this?
I think just enough… is the key to… explain the balance you mean
⏱
Suggested timing: Step 1 → 2 min per card · Step 2 → 1 min per statement · Step 3 → 1–2 min per starter. Total: approx. 20–25 min.
