Exercise 1 — Choose the correct quantifier
Select the best option to complete each sentence. Pay attention to whether the noun is countable or uncountable.
too many too little a few enough hardly any several just enough a little
1. We had guests at the show — there were hundreds of people and it was impossible to move.
2. I had sleep last night — maybe two hours. I'm exhausted.
3. The band has recorded albums over the past decade — I think four or five.
4. There were chairs for everyone — exactly ten people, exactly ten chairs.
5. She has experience in music production, so she still needs to learn a lot.
Exercise 2 — Find and correct the mistake
Each sentence has ONE mistake with a quantifier. The wrong word is in italics. Type the correct word in the box.
1. We don't have many time — the recording session starts in five minutes.
"Time" is uncountable → use much, not many. We use many only with countable nouns.
2. There were a little musicians at the festival — at least thirty bands.
"Musicians" is countable (plural) → use a few, not a little. We use a little only with uncountable nouns.
3. Hardly some people came to the open mic night — it was really disappointing.
The correct collocation is hardly any. "Hardly some" doesn't exist in English.
4. The producer has too much ideas — we need to focus on just a few.
"Ideas" is countable → use too many, not too much. We use too much only with uncountable nouns.
Exercise 3 — Complete the sentences
Finish each sentence in your own words. The quantifier is given in bold. There's no single correct answer — focus on using the quantifier correctly and write something true for you!
1. I have too many _____________________ in my life right now.
2. There are hardly any _____________________ in my neighbourhood.
3. I never have enough _____________________ to _____________________.
4. I know a few people who _____________________.
5. Several of my _____________________ recently _____________________.
6. There's too little _____________________ in the music industry today.

✏️ Write your answers in your notebook or share them with your teacher for feedback.

Exercise 4 — Translation challenge
Translate the sentences from Portuguese into English. Use the quantifier shown in parentheses. More than one correct answer may be possible.
1. Eu tenho poucos alunos esta semana. (a few)
2. Quase ninguém apareceu para o workshop. (hardly any)
3. Não temos tempo suficiente para terminar o curso. (enough)
4. Há músicos demais tentando a mesma coisa. (too many)
